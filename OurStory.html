Our Story

Madi's Version

I like to think that we wouldn't be here today if it weren't for me. Back in February of 2020 - February 7th to be exact - I was at Walla Walla University for the Choir and Orchestra Festival, and had stopped at the cafeteria for lunch. Out of the blue, I saw Edson walking across the cafeteria, which was totally and completely random. I hadn't seen him since I moved from Texas in 2014, so that plus the fact that seeing your childhood crush always has a little adrenaline associated with it, meant that I was completely thrown. So, to avoid coming across as a total fool and since he probably wouldn't recognize 18-year-old me, I decided to deploy my parents, whom he would definitely recognize and who would actually remember his name. Once I had decided that they had conversed for an appropriate amount of time, I went over to say hi, which resulted in a somewhat awkward hug. It wasn't too terrible of an interaction though, so I dm'ed him a couple weeks later on Instagram. My excuse was that I wanted to catch up with a friend from my old church in Texas, but I'm pretty sure my subconscious was just trying to get him to notice me. We talked about how lovely the mangoes are from the Valley, and then The Princess Bride. And then we just didn't stop talking.

The COVID-19 pandemic definitely played into our near constant conversations. I got stuck at home in mid-March, so I had way too much time on my hands, and Edson stayed at Walla Walla during spring quarter but most of his friends had left, so he also needed someone to talk to. We were really just good friends at this point: I didn't think I had any feelings for him, and besides, we were living very different lives anyway. Fast-forward to mid-June, however, and it seems like we might actually have feelings for each other. Since I was going to work for my grandparents in Tri-Cities for a week and he was still in Walla Walla, I suggested that we get together while I was in town, which was going to be the first time we had seen each other since February. He agreed, and we made plans for Wednesday, June 24th, to have a picnic up on the hill with the windmills just off Wallula Junction. The night before, while we were talking on the phone, was the first time he told me he loved me, but I refused to say it back since we hadn't even seen each other in four months, and I wanted to be sure that I actually wanted to commit myself to this person. Turns out, I did, since about two hours into our picnic the next day we were so comfortable around each other that it felt like we had known each other forever, prompting me to ask him, "So...can we just be dating?" I probably couldn't ask for a better first date.

Unfortunately, we immediately started out as a long distance couple, since he was living in Walla Walla that summer and I was still living in Vancouver with my parents. That meant our second date wasn't until a couple weeks later, when he came to my house to visit. Looking back, I'm a little impressed that our relationship survived the weekend. There were just a few stressors we had to deal with: He was "re-meeting" my parents in the context of being my boyfriend (which I'm pretty sure is always a little nerve-wracking), we went on a hard hike up Silver Star Mountain (I'm a little embarrassed to say I was probably a little whiny at some points so I'm glad he could put up with me), and HE ALMOST GOT US STUCK ON THE WAY DOWN IN A HUGE MUD PUDDLE (this one needs no explanation).

We could finally be a normal couple once I went to school that fall, though we had to get really creative with our dates given the COVID restrictions that were still in place. We went on lots of drives, had lots of movie nights at his apartment, and discovered how much we loved star-gazing together. I was really thankful to have him around since I wasn't allowed to have a roommate that first quarter, and social events didn't exist. I would have gone absolutely bonkers without someone I could talk to face-to-face without a mask. We even got to take fall quarter Western Thought together in a weird twist of fate!

Our fun together (at least in person) continued through my sophomore year, until Edson decided to interview with Boeing on a whim. He wasn't expecting to get a job offer, but when he did, I knew that if I told him he shouldn't go because I wanted him to stay I'd be holding him back, it might have been more detrimental to our relationship than if he had gone. So, he accepted the job, and we went to look for apartments over Memorial Weekend of 2022. After looking at apartments we planned to stay Sunday night in the parking lot at Beckler Peak, hike the trail early the next morning, then book it back to Walla Walla so I could be ready to go back to class on Tuesday. Mostly, that plan went without a hitch. We found Edson an apartment and a roommate, explored Leavenworth on Sunday, made it to the trailhead, and got a good night's rest. Our hike up Beckler Peak was when it got a little rough: two miles into the four mile one-way trip we encountered an unexpected field of snow, which we were able to navigate mostly successfully with the GPS on my phone and our strap-on shoe spikes, but then my phone was too close to dying for comfort, so we decided to turn around. Right around then, though, we saw a guy trail-blazing straight up the side of the mountain (instead of attempting to follow the switch-backs like we were), so we asked if we could follow him, which he graciously allowed. Somehow, we made it to the top, so Edson and I were able to stop shortly into our trek down for the picture that Edson had said he wanted to get. He made the mistake, however, of not telling our guide-friend that we were stopping, so I was anxiously poking at the snow with my poles and telling him to hurry up. I had turned around to distract myself with looking at the snow on the trees, and when I turned back around, Edson was down on one knee, asking me if I would marry him. My immediate response was, "Are you kidding??" In my defense, we had explicitly discussed that I didn't want to get married until after I graduated, and he didn't want an engagement longer than nine months, so I was not at ALL expecting it. Obviously, once the shock wore off a litte I was able to yes, and it felt like I was floating the four miles down the mountain. Our day was far from over though: we knew Edson's fog-lights had somehow stayed on too long, draining his car battery, but we both had brought extra battery packs so we weren't worried when we got down to the parking lot. We did start to get worried, though, when the car battery just wouldn't charge, giving us a pretty good hint that his alternator was out. Thanks to a fellow hiker, we were able to get just enough juice to get out of the parking lot and start coasting down the windy road to the main road. Unfortunately, we rolled to a stop about a mile from the main road, and quickly discovered that since it was a holiday, there wasn't going to be many tow trucks to come get us, and AAA wouldn't tow us until we were on actual pavement. It wasn't until after lots of phone calls, two tow truck rides, too many hours, and the wonderful kindness of the Boggess family that we were able to crash into bed.

Then comes a very extended, long distance engagement (which I wouldn't recommend for wedding planning), so I am understandably, REALLY excited for our wedding!!!

Edson's Version

Spring 2020

You're probably wondering how I ended up in this situation. It all started when I went to the Walla Walla University (WWU) cafeteria for the first time my senior year. I was there to meet a professor about an internship opportunity he knew about. There I was attempting to get the details as others came and went, interrupting him to say hello. Finally getting his undivided attention we spoke, only for me to receive a tap on the shoulder from behind. Turning around, I looked up to see a familiar face, which I had last seen nearly a decade ago and 2000+ miles away. A face who I would one day call my father-in-law (Chad Carlton for those wondering). Taking pause with my professor to speak to the Carlton's (Sher being by his side), I soon learned they were chaperoning their eldest daughter...Now, two things first: A) Due to our 3.5 year age gap (I her senior) & her family moving out of Texas when Madi was 12, there never was ANY hint of romantic chemistry when we last knew each other. B) Note her age when I last saw her and that her parents are nearly an entire subway sandwich in height difference and you will easily understand the following surprises...When Chad/Sher mentioned they were joining Madi, I asked if she was at the cafeteria with them and in unison they both stepped to the side creating a opening for me to see Madi standing behind them...only I didn't see her at first. There was no one at the height of her parents, so looking down, lower and lower until below my own head level I saw Madi, who now captured my attention in ways I would have never anticipated those years ago. Madi reached out to me later and we began to catch up. In short time, Madi and I spoke endlessly night after night, and during the day as the days allowed.

Fall 2020 - Winter 2022

At the start of our first date to the Windmills I met her grandparents, and after our second (a hike through fields of flowers), I joined Madi and her family for supper. Several months later, Madi came to WWU to start college, and our relationship continued to grow. So much so that I had intentions of staying in the Walla Walla Valley long enough for her to graduate and us to wed. That worked well enough until a larger opportunity presented itself to me in the form of two recruiter's from Boeing. Long story short, it was an opportunity too big to pass up. Unfortunately, this meant my plans to stay in Walla Walla would have to come to an end.

Spring 2022

There were many ideas I had for my proposal to Madi. One important feature was I wanted to propose AWAY from Walla Walla. Somewhere unique to us and with an ideal backdrop for awesome pictures. Having planned my proposal for the coming months, I realized the perfect excuse to go to the Seattle area was for me to find my new apartment. After meeting my new roommate & seeing several options, Madi and I visited Leavenworth, then backtracked to Skykomish to prepare for the big hike the next day [Memorial Day, remember...this will be important]. In the morning we began our ascent. Unfortunately, the weather proved less than ideal as the sky was grey and the clouds descended covering our views. Nearly turning back, we were met by a stranger who helped us get to the top. I had previously prompted Madi for us to take pictures at the top. As our guide began his descent, I pulled Madi back for us to get our "pictures." Trying to get the camera set up and hide the ring box from her proved...difficult. I was successful in surprising her. Guess what her first response was, "Are you kidding!?"...lol. We kissed (she did eventually say yes) and made our way down to the car. That day also proved...difficult, given that my 4Runner, who had otherwise proven itself reliable decided it no longer wanted a working alternator. When you're miles up a forest road at a trailhead, one might want a working vehicle. We did eventually make it almost to the main road, but after MANY attempts to get help through various means (don't forget it is MEMORIAL DAY), we ended up paying the HEFTY fee to get towed 1 mile back so that AAA could tow us the rest of the 200+ miles. We finally made it where we needed and got to bed past 1 am. Needless to say, I did not make it back to work on time the day after Memorial Day.

Summer 2024

This finally brings us to today, excited to say forever "I do".
